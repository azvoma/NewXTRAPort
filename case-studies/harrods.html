Harrods Pre-Order Feature Design

Effortless, luxury-grade way to reserve high-demand products. Designed a luxury-grade pre-order experience allowing customers to reserve products before official release — improving the shopping experience while enabling Harrods to forecast demand more accurately and manage inventory with confidence.

The Challenge

"I missed out on the limited-edition bag I wanted because I didn't know when it would be available. By the time I heard about it, everything was sold out. There should be a way to reserve items in advance."

— Emma Thompson, Luxury Shopper

Harrods did not offer a pre-order capability across its website or mobile app. Through early analysis, I identified this gap as a key driver of customer frustration and missed revenue, particularly for high-demand and limited-edition products.

  • Customers frequently missed high-demand items due to lack of early access
  • No digital mechanism existed to capture demand before launch
  • Customers were forced to repeatedly check availability, increasing frustration
  • Missed conversion opportunities reduced both revenue and loyalty

Design Process

01Research

Used a mixed-method approach: customer surveys and interviews revealing strong demand for guaranteed access and transparency; user journey analysis mapping limited-edition purchase journeys to identify friction points; competitive analysis of how luxury retailers handle pre-ordering; and business impact assessment of how pre-ordering could improve inventory planning.

02Insights

Synthesised research around three pillars: security (confidence in reservation), simplicity (effortless flow), and exclusivity (service-oriented, not transactional). The solution needed to feel like a luxury service, not a standard ecommerce feature. Anchored design decisions around a high-value luxury shopper persona.

03Ideation

Generated multiple concepts using Crazy 8s and rapid sketching, evaluated against user impact, technical feasibility, luxury experience standards, and WCAG 2.2 AA compliance. Designed end-to-end user flows ensuring the pre-order journey felt familiar, predictable, and integrated into Harrods' existing experience.

04Solution & Validation

Delivered a mobile-first, accessible experience balancing luxury aesthetics with clarity and usability. Low-fidelity wireframes validated core user flows before moving to high-fidelity design. 100% task completion in usability testing. Users consistently described the experience as "intuitive" and "premium".
