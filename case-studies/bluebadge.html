Blue Badge Digital Companion

A complete redesign of the Blue Badge scheme digital service for 2.5M eligible users in England — streamlining access to disabled parking permits through GDS-compliant, WCAG 2.2 AA accessible design that cut incomplete applications by 40%.

The paper trail was failing people

The existing Blue Badge application process was paper-heavy, confusing, and inaccessible — the very people it was designed to help were the most likely to struggle with it. Drop-off rates were high, support queries were overwhelming call centres, and many eligible applicants never completed their application at all.

The challenge was to redesign the end-to-end digital service using GDS principles and the GOV.UK Design System — building something genuinely usable by people with a wide range of physical and cognitive disabilities, from the first touchpoint to the final confirmation.

Understanding the people behind the application

Before designing anything, I ran a structured discovery phase: reviewing existing analytics, shadowing phone support agents, and conducting contextual interviews with applicants across three eligibility categories. The goal was to understand not just what people did, but why they stopped.

Themes from research

  • Users with visual impairments couldn't navigate existing forms without assistance
  • Medical evidence requirements were unclear — applicants didn't know what to upload
  • Session timeouts lost progress mid-application with no recovery mechanism
  • Confirmation and status updates were inconsistent, driving repeat contact to support

From structure to high-fidelity — built on GDS foundations

01Information architecture and flow mapping

Mapped the full service architecture across four eligibility paths, identifying where conditional logic needed to branch and where users could be guided progressively rather than shown everything at once.

02Low-fidelity wireframes — GOV.UK patterns applied

Low-fidelity wireframes established the core question-per-page pattern, progressive disclosure of eligibility criteria, and the evidence upload journey — validated early with users before any visual polish was applied.

03WCAG 2.2 AA — every criterion evidenced

Focus management on page transitions, colour contrast ratios verified at 4.5:1 minimum, visible persistent labels on all inputs, error identification in text not colour alone, session timeout warnings at 2 minutes remaining with extension option, and descriptive alt text on all images.

04High-fidelity screens built on GOV.UK Design System

Final designs used GOV.UK Design System components throughout. Custom components were only introduced where no existing pattern covered the need, and each was documented for the pattern library.
