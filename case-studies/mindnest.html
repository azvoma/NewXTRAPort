MindNest

35% reduction in week-one churn through improved onboarding. Redesigned the onboarding and habit formation experience for a wellness app, achieving a 35% reduction in week-one churn and 22% increase in daily active users.

The Problem

52% of users churned within the first 7 days, before experiencing the app's core value proposition. New users felt overwhelmed by options and unclear about how to build a consistent wellness routine.

Design Process

01Discovery & Insights

Analysed cohort data to identify churn patterns. Conducted diary studies with 8 users over 2 weeks. Found that users who set reminders had 3× better retention. Developed three key user archetypes with goals and barriers, and mapped behavioural patterns leading to drop-off.

02Ideation & Exploration

Explored habit formation frameworks (BJ Fogg, Nir Eyal). Sketched 15 different onboarding approaches. Prioritised a progressive goal-setting model and designed a trigger-routine-reward cycle to support long-term habit formation.

03Design & Prototype

Created personalised onboarding based on user goals. Designed a streak system and gentle reminders. Built an iOS prototype with micro-animations covering the complete onboarding and home experience, along with animation specs for developers.

04Testing & Iteration

Ran beta test with 50 users over 2 weeks. Tracked daily engagement and completion rates. Simplified goal-setting based on confusion feedback and produced a summary iteration report with key findings.

Results & Impact

Retention (up from 48%)

Users also began sharing their streaks on social media, creating organic growth through peer accountability.

Key Learnings

🔔

Push notification timing

Would have tested push notification timing more rigorously for optimal engagement.

👫

Social features matter

Could have added more social features earlier to increase accountability.

🎉

Celebration moments

Matter more than gamification points — small wins keep users motivated.

🎯

Small, achievable goals

Are key to habit formation — don't let users set unrealistic expectations.
